Retail Work

I began work with a local grocer in Omaha, NE in the deli. After moving up to a “back office” position, I was offered a job at another grocery chain -- Wild Oats. This began my first foray into the world of natural and organic grocery stores. During this time, I was still trying to figure out what I wanted to do as an adult, but I never lost my passion for programming.

In the spring of 2005, Whole Foods announced it was going to open a store in Omaha. Whole Foods was a huge competitor for Wild Oats, but offered much better benefits. One of my managers at Wild Oats took a job with Whole Foods and offered me a buyer position there because he was impressed with my previous work. At the time, I believed the position would give me an opportunity to save money and work towards returning to school. One of the best things about working with Whole Foods was also one of the worst: The pay was solid and there were enough opportunities for growth that I ended up putting off returning to school while I climbed the “corporate ladder.” During the time at Omaha, I worked my way up to an associate team leader position and helped in different capacities storewide at other locations region-wide. When I was informed that a produce team leader position (produce manager) was available at a new Whole Foods store in Lincoln, I jumped at the opportunity.

Moving to Lincoln was arguably one of the best decisions I have ever made. Not only did I receive a promotion and a brand new set of skills, it also forced me to grow as a person. My professional and personal growth helped me become the confident adult I am today. After roughly a year of the store being open, I applied for a promotion to associate store team leader (effectively assistant store manager). At the time I had done everything I could do as a team leader and was looking for more of a challenge.
